What is it?

People of color essentially means anybody who is not White or from a European heritage or doesn't identify themselves in this racial category. Since it has been use more to describe people who aren't racially white, this makes our society feel more of a threat. From the artifacts that I have analyze before, all three artifacts have discuss of the term being a growing problem. Since the negativity of the term has an impact in our society, the three artifacts talk about different situations where "people of color" are attacked because of their race. Below you will find the three artifacts that I have analyze this quarter.

Why are we talking about People of Color?

In my English class, we are discussing about different terms that deals with racial and social issues that America is facing. Specifically, I have chosen the topic of "people of color" because I, myself, am someone who identifies as a person of color. Growing up, I have always been surrounded by people of color until coming into high school where it was a predominately White school. I think it is a great way to show an identification of who we are and what our culture is.

What do I think about the term?

People of color has been a word that has been around for centuries dating back to when slavery will still an acceptable act in our country. Currently, it is more used as a negative term than a positive connation. When I see people use the term, it is an act of unification. This is a time where many people may either feel excluded from society or unifying the country together. Whichever one it is, we can see a divde in how our world feels about racial and/or social topics.

The Journey From 'Colored' To 'Minorities' To 'People Of Color' discusses about the term "People of Color" and how it manifests into today society. In summary of the article, it highlights the idea of people of color with historical facts and many different anecdotes. She emphasizes that the term has had a long history where it was used during slavery or even during the times of Martin Luther King Jr. However, her main point was how the term "people of color" transform into other different versions of words. It was first said as "colored," then still used today as "people of color," but lastly, "minority." In the article, Kee Malesky points out that the word "colored" has a better positive vibe than actually being called "Black." That statement is what I believe is true since when calling someone by their race seems way more inappropiate than calling someone "colored." However, some may argue that they want to be known for their color and be called "Black, Asian, Hispanic, etc." and this is what the term "people of color" can have for many people. They want to be identified and be known for who they are which is special and reassuring that they want others to acknowledge their backgrounds and culture since it is something people are proud of. For me, I agree with this because I want people to see that I am an Asian American man and not someone who is just a "colored" person. Being called "colored" is very broad and not specifying or describe who I am as a person which is something I do not agree with. Lastly, she gives a small anecdote about what she learned in an art class when she was younger since she was taught that color crayons were the term shouldn't exist or that it is irrelevant to the subject. Her argument is something I want to point out because I believe bringing up the term show a division in our society which doesn't help unity. The term not only disregards this theme of being together but it is counterargues that the word is not what we should care about but being together. Although this argument sound valid, I think the term "person of color" can necessarily be a good thing since it helps us become different from others, allowing us to become an individual with custom cultures and backgrounds which very much disagrees with Malesky.
